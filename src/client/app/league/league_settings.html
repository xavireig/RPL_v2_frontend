RPL Cup Settings
Cup Type Scoring Fixtures
In-Season

If you choose this option, your league will play an FA Cup-style knockout tournament. All teams from your league are entered and play in a qualifying round, followed by unseeded knockout rounds until a champion is determined.

Post-Season

If you choose this option, your league will play a playoffs-style tournament, where teams qualify for the Cup via their league season finish. Your league chairman can select and seed the teams and matches are played after your league season has concluded.

In-Season

Matches are scored using a fixed points system, designed to prevent draws from occuring. Scoring for In-Season Cups is not currently customizable.

Eight Team Knockout Stage

In leagues with eight teams, all teams advance to the Quarterfinal Round. (l.e. no Qualifying Round is played.)

In leagues with ten or twelve teams, eight teams advance to the Quarterfinals by placing in the top eight of the Qualifying Round.

Sixteen Team Knockout Stage

If your league has two or more divisions linked for promotion/relegation, you can select this option to merge divisions for the RPL Cup. All teams will play in one Qualifying Round, with the top sixteen point tallies advancing to the Round of 16.

Note: all linked divisions leagues must select this option in their RPL Cup settings.

In-Season

- FA Cup-style knockout tournament

- All teams from your league qualify.

- If your league is linked to another division through promotion/relegation, you can choose to have all teams across all divisions play in your RPL Cup.

- Cups with 10 or more teams first play the Qualifying Round; no head-to-head mathes rather the top 8 (0r 16 if leagues are linked) advance to the Knockout Stage.

- Matches are scored using a fixed points system, designed to prevent draws as well to produce slightly different "form" between League matches and Cup matches.

Post-Season

- NBA Playoffs-style tournament

- Teams qualify for for the Cup via their league season finish, after season has concluded

- You can set the number of teams who qualify for the Cup - 4 or 6 - but cannot play across linked leagues

- Matches are scored using the same scoring system as your regular season
